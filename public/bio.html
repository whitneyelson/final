About Me

Craig A. Elson is an American photographer based in Los Angeles, California who has traveled to more than 60 countries throughout Africa, Asia, South America, Europe and the Caribbean over the past 30 years. He is focused primarily on wildlife, landscape, and aerial photography and is happiest when shooting in golden light anywhere in Africa.

Craig first fell in love with photography during an around the world travel adventure while in college on an experiential learning program called Semester at Sea. Borrowing his father's Nikon 2020 film camera and a pair of zoom lenses, he set sail in the autumn of 1988 determined to document his journey on film. Craig subsequently spent the summer of 1990 following his graduation from college independently traveling throughout eastern and southern Africa, mostly on safari, this time with the newer Nikon 4004s. As has happened to many who have traveled there before him, Africa and its wildlife managed to deeply embed themselves in Craig's soul and not a day goes by without his thoughts turning to his memories and/or visions of future experiences there. He then returned to "real life" after that summer, embarking upon a career in the international media business followed by private equity and venture capital investing which afforded him the opportunity to travel the world some more, both for business and for adventure. His wanderlust grew and his camera was always by his side. But the realities of a busy professional and family life eventually set in and photography became mostly a fond memory for nearly fifteen years.

Craig's love for photography reignited in the summer of 2016 on his first trip to Africa since 1998. The siren's song of safari and some upgraded Canon gear, coupled with the growing technological wonders of digital, ignited a newfound passion for his craft. Photography has been part of Craig's life every day since returning from this three week trip where he was able to introduce his three daughters to Africa for the first time. The travel bug was stirred anew, and many adventures, both domestic and international, have taken place these past several years. He switched over to shooting Sony in early 2020 and is loving the incredible auto focus system in the a9ii and the details brought out by the 61mp of the a7r4, not to mention the freedom that mirrorless gives you to handhold even the largest of lenses. And now, of course, the game changing Sony a1 awaits delivery. In these past few years, Craig has taught himself Lightroom and Photoshop for post processing and is currently enamored with the wizardry of Topaz Labs as well.
